OurVision
The Vision of OGM is to prepare globally competent and well-cultured citizens.
OurMission
We at OGM have the mission to impart quality education inspired by progressive education models. We equally value our traditional cultures that includes respect, morality and responsibility. Parents and other stakeholders remain as partners in materializing the Mission.
AboutOur School

Established in 2051 BS (1995 AD) Om Gyan Mandir (OGM) School has proven to be a trusted institution for academic and overall growth of its students. With the change of time, we are keenly preparing our students for challenges and opportunities that lie ahead into their future.

We have the motto of Learning to Lead. We have this motto because future is going to be even more complicated. Only people with leaderships kills who have the ability to adapt to challenging circumstances, build a shared vision and lead teams can contribute to societies and can excel in their careers. At OGM, we are working to design experiences for students to deal with challenges and work in teams. We also offer students platform to value our rich cultural traditions and at the same time equip our students with contemporary technological skills.

Our leadership team, teaching faculty and entire staff members are committed to materialize the Vision, Mission and Objectives of the school.

My school days experience

Education is not just about going to school and getting a certificates. It's about widening our knowledge and absorbing the truth.

School is said to be a temple of knowledge, the very first place that introduces you to the world and more so our own self. My school life is about the memories I deeply cherish. The memories of hard work, fun, lifelong friendship, and extracurricular activities. I still remember the year 2005 in which I had won three national awards in art competition and was able to flourish my school's name nationally and later honored as THE BEST STUDENT OF THE YEAR. And I still remember my first photo and name quoted with inspiring words on the National Broadsheet. It was as an honour of my Honesty, Dedication and Love of Learning to Lead.

I am a resident doctor in Ophthalmology and doing well in my profession. There are so many helping hands beside my achievements and the vital is of course my teachers. They have made me what I am today and given various opportunities to develop not only my scholastic abilities but also the entire extracurricular activities which helped me to understand how to behave socially and helped me build a bold and confident personality. I owe to my teachers for all those achievements I have made. Thank you

Dr. Priyanka Singh

Resident doctor (ophthalmology)
Gandaki Medical College, Pokhara
